The park was a canvas of golden leaves, swirling in the crisp autumn air. Emma fidgeted with her locket, heart racing as she spotted Liam. Their eyes locked, joy bursting forth in their smiles, as if no time had passed. 

“It’s been too long, hasn’t it?” Emma breathed, a hint of nostalgia in her gaze.

“Yeah… I’ve missed this place. And you,” Liam replied, wrapping her in an embrace that lingered just a moment too long, tension crackling between them.

Memories flashed in split-screen; Emma recalled Liam’s childhood promise to always be there, while Liam nervously toyed with a wedding ring hidden in his pocket. 

“Do you ever think about what could have been?” Emma asked, vulnerability in her voice.

“Every day… but I can’t,” Liam hesitated, pain etched across his features.

“I don’t want to lose you again… what if I told you I still love you?” he blurted, eyes wide with longing.

With faces inches apart, Emma whispered, “Then let’s not lose this moment.” Their gazes held a world of unspoken promises, as the scene faded to black.
