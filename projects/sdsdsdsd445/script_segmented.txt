In a vibrant park, the sun beams down on Buster, a goofy dog with boundless energy. His tail wagging like a metronome, he readies himself to impress his owner. “Watch this!” he beams, launching himself after a frisbee, only to trip over a stick, spinning into a pile of leaves. Nearby, lounging on a bench, is Whiskers, an aloof cat with an unimpressed gaze. *Amateur,* her thought bubble reads, as she smirks.

Determined to win her admiration, Buster tries again, but mid-air, Whiskers zips in, snatching the frisbee from behind and landing with grace. “You’ve got to be kidding me!” Buster exclaims, wide-eyed. 

Not one to back down, he declares a fetch-off. Quick cuts show them prepping with exaggerated seriousness—Buster stretching, Whiskers sharpening her claws. 

At the signal, they dash. Buster charges after a new frisbee, but Whiskers takes a shortcut, reaching the hill first, frisbee in paw. “It’s not about the fetch; it’s about the finesse,” she quips, lounging triumphantly.
